Lifestyle

Recycling

Recycling is one of the easiest ways to live sustainably. Recycle plastic water bottles, old glass bottles, or aluminium cans. Keep a recycle bin at your home and try making more trips to nearby recycling stations than to the landfill.

Reduce Water Waste

Every day, you rely on water for a wide variety of uses indoors. You can conserve water by taking shorter showers, staying away from bottled water, fixing leaky toilets, and using low-flow and low-water appliance options.

Reduce Energy Consumption

Saving energy helps you to not only save money, but also to reduce your home’s impact on the environment. There are numerous ways to save energy at home: turning off the lights when leaving the room, using cold water from faucets or when doing laundry, increasing insulation in the house, and practicing energy-eonscious habits.

Quit Disposable Products

Using non-disposable products directly minimizes waste. Stop using single-use plastics, and start using metal bottles, metal or glass containers, cloth napkins, metal utensils, and more. Non-disposable products are healthier, while also being environmental friendly.

Eat Locally

Carbon footprints, air pollution, and dependence on fossil fuels can be avoided by purchasing local and seasonal foods. Local foods are healthier, fresher, and environmentally and economically beneficial for you and your community.

Donate Clothes

Donating clothes has more perks than helping people who can't afford them. Clothing donation reduces landfill and our carbon footprint. It also helps to save water by reducing high production of items that involves inordinate amounts of water. Donating clothes is a great way for you to declutter your house, or getting rid of clothes that don't fit you and unwanted.

Grow Your Own Food

Growing your own food in a community or home garden helps to promote sustainable living in your area. Gardens create green spaces and the garden waste can be returned to support healthy soil. In many areas, green spaces can play a significant role in offsetting carbon emissions.

Take Advantage of Daylight

Sunlight is free. Using sunlight as a natural lightsource helps to reduce dependence on fossil fuels to produce electricity, and your bulbs and tube lights are going to last longer. It also directly lessens the amount of power that you use while you are up.

Drive Less

Travelling to places by walking or biking leads to the reduction of air pollution and the consumption of natural resources. It will also improve your health and reduce the strain on public health resources. If walking and biking are not options, taking public transportation and carpooling are other alternatives that will save you money and reduce air pollution considerably.

Energy

Use Energy-Effient Appliances

Use of energy efficient appliances minimizes the exploitation of natural resources. Saving energy also saves money. By using energy-efficient appliances, your household can save up to $400 per year on utility bills.

Replace Your Lightbulbs

LED lights are up to 80% more efficient than traditional light bulbs such as fluorescent and incandescent lights. LED light bulbs also typically use about 25%-80% less energy than traditional incandescents, saving you money.

Choose Renewable Energy

Choosing renewable energy over fossil fuels is a great way to stop climate change. Install solar panels for solar water heating. You can also try to figure out any way to add clean power to the grid to offset your carbon footprint.

Adjust Your Thermostat

Turning up your air conditioning by several degrees can significant;y reduce your energy bill, while also keeping CO2 out of the air. In the winter, putting on a sweater instead of turning up the heat can also save a hefty amount of energy.

Unplug Your Electronics

Many appliances consume energy even when you’re not using them. Directly unplug electronics or plug items into a surge protector/power strip and turning the whole strip off when you leave a room.

Do Your Laundry in Cold Water

Most of the energy used to wash clothes, approximately 90%, comes from heating the water. Switching to colder water can lower your gas or electric bill. Not only that, cold-water washing can least likely shrink or fade and ruin clothes.

Transportation

Walk for Short Trips

Walking not only reduces your carbon footprint, but it also reduces body fat, lower blood pressure and is free.

Try Cycling

Try switching from driving your car to cycling once in a while. Cycling will burn around 300 calories each way and can save over 300 kilograms of greenhouse gas emissions each year.

Plan Your Journey

So many apps nowadays can help ypu plan out your trips and journeys, so that you can check for the most convenient route or transportation option.

Green-Drive

Driving green means you can save up to 20% in fuel economy. Learn to anticipate stops sooner to avoid braking suddenly. Focus on the long view of the road ahead. Open the windows instead of using air conditioning during the summer

Car-Pool

Car-pooling is a great way to share the costs of fuel and parking, and to break the monotony of your daily commute.

Simply Have a Local Adventure

If you wanna set out on an adventure, try having some fun close to home, instead of communting far distances by car.

Fashion

Shop Vintage

The amount of energy needed to produce vintage/second-hand clothing is zero. Thrifting your clothes can reduce your carbon footpront significantly.

Donate Unwanted Clothes

Donating your unwanted clothes to a good cause, rather than leaving them hanging in your closet will help others to be more sustainable, who will invest in your old pieces, rather than buying something new.

Look After Your Clothes

Start looking after your clothes so that they last longer. Look after them properly and you will have to replace things less often. Try ashing your denim inside out, and go the extra mile to ensure your clothes stay at their best for longer.

Remodel/Upcycle Your Clothes

Try turning your old clothes into something else. When something rips or a heel breaks, you don't necessarily have to throw it away. Learn how to repair your clothes and accessories.

Go for Quality Over Quantity

Buying less high-quality items a year, rather than more cheaper, less eco-friendly pieces will dramatically reduce your carbon footprint. Save up, invest and buy less.

Adjust Your Spending

Instead of splurging on clothing hauls, or any hauls, try investmenting cash on the things you would wear every day.

Products

Stainless Steel Drink Bottles

Switch to metal drinking bottles to ensure a safe, sustainable way to consume your favourite beverages on the go. This is much better and easier than using plastic bottles everyday.

Towels

Start using towels as an alternative for single-use towels or paper towels.

Reusable Grocery Tote Bags

Using reusable grocery bags is a simple way to cut down on a wasteful habit without missing out on convenience. Some grocery stores even offer incentives for people who bring their own bags.

Rechargeable Batteries

Batteries are made up of a very harmful of chemicals, and can cause soil and water pollution.Rechargeable batteries ones solve that problem while also saving you money and space.

Reusable/Metal Straws

Using disposable utensils of any kind can prevent a lot of unnecessary waste into the environment, but straws are a particularly easy fix.

LED Lightbulbs

LEDs are up to 80% more efficient than traditional lighting options. 95% of the energy in LEDs is converted into light and only 5% is wasted as heat. They also use much less energy, which reduces the demand on power plants and decreases the accompanying greenhouse gas emissions.
